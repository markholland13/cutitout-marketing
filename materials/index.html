Material Range

choose the right material for the job

The right material depends on how the part will be used. Strength, corrosion resistance, finish, appearance, and weight all affect the best choice.

Mild Steel

strong, practical, and powder-coat friendly

Available in 1.0 mm, 1.5 mm, 2.0 mm, and 3.0 mm. A dependable choice for brackets, panels, and general fabricated parts.

1.0 mm 1.5 mm 2.0 mm 3.0 mm
Stainless Steel

cleaner corrosion resistance for tougher environments

Available in 1.0 mm, 1.5 mm, 2.0 mm, and 3.0 mm. Best suited where durability and corrosion resistance matter more than lowest cost.

1.0 mm 1.5 mm 2.0 mm 3.0 mm
Aluminium

lighter weight with a cleaner finish path

Available in 1.0 mm and 1.5 mm. A good option for lighter parts where weight matters and a neater visual finish is needed.

1.0 mm 1.5 mm Lightweight
Acrylic

clean cut plastic parts with colour selection

Available in 3.0 mm and 5.0 mm with Black, White, Clear, and Dark Grey Tinted options. Best presented as a simpler, design-led option rather than a metal equivalent.

3.0 mm 5.0 mm Clear Black White Tinted
Choosing A Material

start with the job, then choose the material

Mild steel is usually the most practical option for general fabrication work
Stainless steel is better where corrosion resistance matters
Aluminium is useful where lower weight is important
Acrylic is better suited to display, panel, and signage-style jobs
